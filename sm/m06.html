The Two-Sided Platform

Rochet & Tirole / Eisenmann / Parker & Van Alstyne · two-sided markets, cross-side network effects, chicken-and-egg.

Idea Diagram

The core idea

A platform creates value by enabling two distinct user groups to transact, and each side becomes more attractive as the other grows. Because the demand on one side depends on participation on the other, the strategic problem is not setting one price but choosing a price structure: subsidise the side that draws the other and charge the side that values it most. Get both sides on board, or neither shows up. Network effects, not features, are the moat. — after Rochet & Tirole and Eisenmann

Cross-side dynamics on a two-sided platform

The platform sits between two interdependent user groups. Each side joins because the other is there — and the loop, once ignited, runs on its own.
