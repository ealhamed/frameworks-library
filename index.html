A note, before you click around

I was losing the frameworks almost as fast as we covered them. A good idea on Friday became a vague feeling by Wednesday, and by the next block I couldn't recall the name of the model, only that we had one.

So I built this. It is not a study guide. It is not an official summary of the programme. It is how I make the material stick — one page per module, the core idea on top, the diagram in the middle, the named frameworks I want to remember, and a short “how to actually use this” at the bottom.

If any of it is useful to you, please take it. If any of it is wrong, tell me and I’ll fix it — this is a living notebook, not a finished book.

— Ebrahim, LBS EMBA Dubai 2027

About the object

Why it looks like this.

Each module page is designed to be scannable in under a minute and to reward a longer read. The writing is sparse on purpose. The diagrams are hand-drawn on purpose. The palette is warm on purpose. If in three years I come back tired and need to remember what Congruence or SAID or Cournot actually meant, I want to find everything where I left it.
